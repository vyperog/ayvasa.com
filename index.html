THE COHERENCE PROTOCOL

A biology-grounded meditation protocol for training stability, clarity, and self-regulation.

Nothing to believe.

Nothing to attain.

The full protocol, offered openly.

Panel 2

PREPARATION

Choose a place that feels neutral and repeatable.

Comfort matters more than posture.

Sit or lie down in a way that does not require monitoring.

Allow the body to stop adjusting.

Session length: 5–10 minutes.

End before fatigue appears.

Panel 3 • Phase 0

PHASE 0 — BASELINE BREATH RESET

Allow awareness to rest lightly on the breath.

Do not control the breath.

Do not deepen it.

Do not regulate pace.

Stay with natural breathing for 6–12 cycles

or until the breath begins to feel unremarkable.

This phase establishes baseline regulation.

Panel 4 • Phase 1

PHASE 1 — MICRO-RESONANCE ENTRY

Let awareness include:

  • The breath
  • The body as a whole

Do not narrow attention.

Do not search for sensation.

Remain here for 1–2 minutes.

If attention drifts, return gently.

This phase supports initial coherence.

Panel 5 • Phase 2

PHASE 2 — COHERENCE COMPRESSION

Allow the breath to soften naturally.

If a pause appears between breaths,

let it remain without holding.

Do not extend the pause.

Do not manage timing.

Stay here for 1–3 minutes.

Ease matters more than duration.

Panel 6 • Phase 3

PHASE 3 — RESONANT SWEEP

This is attention-only. No breath multitasking.

Palate → Sinuses → Brow → Crown → Heart

Move lightly through the path, one location at a time.

If perception feels vague or faint, continue anyway.

Remain for 2–4 minutes.

Panel 7 • Phase 4

PHASE 4 — THE THRESHOLD WINDOW

Allow attention to rest in the subtle internal gap

often sensed behind the upper palate.

Do not focus tightly.

Do not try to enter anything.

Let experience remain open and unforced.

Typical duration: 2–6 minutes.

Panel 8

END / GROUNDING

Before moving on:

  • Open the eyes
  • Notice contact with the floor or chair
  • Take one or two natural breaths

Return to activity without evaluating the session.

Carryover matters more than experience.

Panel 9

A FINAL NOTE

This protocol does not create coherence.

It removes interference.

If practice feels ordinary, stay close.

If less feels sufficient, trust that.

Stability grows quietly.
