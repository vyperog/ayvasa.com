THE COHERENCE PROTOCOL

Nothing to believe. Nothing to attain.

The Coherence Protocol is a biology-grounded meditation protocol for nervous system regulation, stability, and clarity. Rather than aiming for altered states or spiritual experiences, it trains functional coherence—how breath, attention, sensation, and the body organize and regulate together.

The full meditation protocol is offered openly. No belief system. No hierarchy. No promises of awakening or transformation. Simply a clear, step-by-step practice you can explore at your own pace. Let the panels guide you, or move through them manually. This is an invitation to experience coherence directly, in your own nervous system.

The full protocol, offered openly.

2-6 Minutes

PREPARATION

Choose a quiet place that feels neutral and repeatable. Comfort matters more than posture.

Sit or lie down in a way that does not require monitoring. Allow the body to stop adjusting.

Rest the tongue lightly on the palate, just behind the upper front teeth. No pressure. No effort.

A simple internal whisper can help organize attention: "I am preparing the field."

~2 Minutes

PHASE 0 — BASELINE BREATH RESET

Allow awareness to rest lightly on the breath. Do not control the breath. Do not deepen it. Do not regulate pace.

Stay with natural breathing for 12–16 cycles or until breath begins to feel unremarkable.

This phase establishes baseline regulation.

~4 Minutes

PHASE 1 — MICRO-RESONANCE ENTRY

Inhale through the nose for 4 seconds. Exahle for 6 seconds with a gentle hum: "mmm." The hum should be low volume.

Repeat for 20 to 24 rounds.

Keep the tongue resting on the palate. Let attention rest in the resonance—face, skull, chest—without analysis. No story. Just felt sensation.

This phase supports initial coherence.

~6 Minutes

PHASE 2 — COHERENCE COMPRESSION

Inhale for 4 seconds. Exhale for 6 seconds.

After the exhale, allow a gentle pause—approximately 2 seconds. This is not a hold. It's simply the momemt where brreath has finished and nothing replaces it yet.

Repeat for 30 rounds.

If the pause feels strained, shorten it. If it disappears naturally, let it disappear.

3 Minutes

PHASE 3 — RESONANT SWEEP

This is attention-only. No multitasking.

Palate → Sinuses → Brow → Crown → Heart

Move lightly through the path, one location at a time, 1-2 seconds each. Whisper internally: "open." Then release immediately and move on.

If perception feels vague or faint, continue anyway.

2-6 Minutes

PHASE 4 — THE THRESHOLD WINDOW

This is the stillness phase. Non-doing.

Drop effort. Rest attention at the subtle internal space often felt as the "gap" behind the palate. This is not the tongue-on-palate point. It's a quieter space behind it.

Allow whatever arises to rise and fall without reaction—thoughts, images, emotions, sensation.

Do not try to maintain a state. Do not try to stay in stillness. If interference appears, let it. If still appears, let it be ordinary.

3 Minutes

GROUNDING

Return to gentle humming on the exhale for 10 to 15 breath cycles.

Then wiggle hands and feet. Take one slow inhale, then a long exhale. Open the eyes. Re-enter activity without commentary.

Grounding is not optional. It supports integration. Without it, the transition back into activity can feel abrupt or disorienting.

A FINAL NOTE

This protocol does not create coherence. It helps to reduce or remove interference.

If practice feels ordinary, stay close. If less feels sufficient, trust that.

Stability grows quietly.
